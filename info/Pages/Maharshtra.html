    Fashion in Maharashtra, a western state in India, is a vibrant and diverse blend of traditional and contemporary styles. Maharashtra is home to the bustling city of Mumbai, often referred to as the fashion capital of India, which greatly influences the fashion scene in the state.


    History

    Historically, the women of the Maratha Empire showcased their abilities and caliber by assisting their fellow male warriors during the times of war. Hence, in order to accomplish their mission and yet maintain their comfort of physical movement during the wars, these Marathi women invented this Maharashtrian style of drape.


    For Womens:

    Navari


    Traditional Maharashtrian attire reflects the rich cultural heritage of the region. The iconic nine-yard saree known as the Navari or Nauvari saree is a significant part of Maharashtrian fashion. It is draped in a unique way, with the saree tucked at the back, allowing for greater freedom of movement. The saree is typically paired with traditional Maharashtrian jewelry such as the nath (nose ring), chandrakor bindi (moon-shaped forehead decoration), and Kolhapuri saaj


    Sarre


    The Sarre is a traditional Maharashtrian saree worn An additional significance of the sari is to symbolize national pride in India. For the sake of preserving Indian culture, it may be obligatory for girls to wear the sari. Women in India wear the saree as a symbol of pride in their nation and culture.


    Paithani

    Paithani is a sari made of silk and zari. It is a plain weave, with weft figuring designs according to the principles of tapestry. Traditionally, Paithanis had a coloured, cotton muslin field that often contained considerable supplementary zari patterning. However, in the 19th century, silk fields were also woven.


    For Mens:

    The men in rural Maharashtra are seen wearing a Dhotar or Dhoti. A Dhoti is a single piece of cloth tucked around the waist. Dhoti covers the entire leg till the ankle. The colour of Dhotis usually ranges from saffron, cream or white.

    Overall, fashion in Maharashtra is a dynamic blend of tradition and innovation. From traditional Maharashtrian attire to contemporary designs inspired by global trends, the state offers a diverse range of fashion choices. With its rich cultural heritage, thriving fashion industry, and a growing focus on sustainability, Maharashtra continues to make a significant contribution to the fashion landscape in India and beyond.
