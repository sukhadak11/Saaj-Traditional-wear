    • Indo-Western

    Western wear is a clothing style derived from 19th century American old west region. Jeans, t-shirts, and jackets are the prevalent clothing forms of this style among men and women. This category of clothes is widely accepted by many cultures, as they offer comfort, durability, and variety.



    History

    In 2012, Goan designer Wendell Rodricks stated that “Indo-western clothing originated in Goa.” On December 31, 1600, Queen Elizabeth I formally allowed trade in the East Indies to The Company of Merchants of London Trading into the East Indies. This was a strategic move to compete in the Dutch controlled spice trade.
